Drag Queen Brenda Bressed Surprises The Coaches During 'The Voice AU' Blind Auditions

As Season 12 of The Voice Australia continues its Blind Auditions, a diverse lineup of musical talents have shown up to compete for the top spot. Among these artists is the sensational drag queen Brenda Bressed, who recently stole the spotlight during the Blind Auditions.

Brenda Bressed Made a Successful Blind Audition on The Voice AU

Brenda has an established presence in the drag scene. She wowed the audience and coaches alike with her electrifying performance on The Voice AU, setting the stage ablaze with her unique interpretation of Cher’s “Strong Enough.”

The performance not only had the audience bopping along but also prompted coaches Rita Ora and Guy Sebastian to turn their chairs, eager to have Brenda on their teams. Her ability to reshape the song to fit her style and key impressed both the coaches and the viewers.

After wowing the audience and coaches, Brenda faced the tough decision of choosing between two renowned coaches, who recognized her potential. In a moment that added to the suspense of the show, Brenda announced her decision to join Team Rita Ora.

As Brenda continues her journey on The Voice AU, audiences can expect more captivating performances and dazzling moments. With her passion for drag and her exceptional stage presence, Brenda is undoubtedly a force to be reckoned with in the world of entertainment.

Brenda Bressed’s Song Choice Held Significant Meaning To Her

The Blind Auditions are always a pivotal moment for contestants, and Brenda shared her feelings about stepping onto the stage in a recent podcast interview. Brenda also mentioned that she couldn’t help but smile when she saw the crowd’s enthusiastic reaction to her drag appearance.

The choice of Cher’s “Strong Enough” held personal significance for Brenda. The song had been part of her journey into drag, as she explained during the podcast interview.

“It was in the playlist of songs, and because I was still new to the scene, I wasn’t all that familiar with all of the camp classics and everything,” Brenda said. “I fell in love with ‘Strong Enough’ doing Lip Sync Roulette.”

Brenda’s ability to engage the audience went beyond the coaches’ chairs, reaching the entire crowd. She believes in giving her best performance regardless of the outcome and focuses on entertaining the audience.
